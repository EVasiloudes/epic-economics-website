**[Epic Economics Draft, 13 March 2025]{.underline}**

***Overview***

*How does your breakfast make your way to your table? Why do you have an
imported car? Who creates value? Why do we have recessions? What's more
important: growth of the economy or equality of income and wealth and
opportunity? Why did JK Galbraith say that the only function of economic
forecasting is to "make astrology look respectable\"? Why did Joan
Robinson argue that the purpose of studying economics is \"to avoid
being deceived by economists.\" Economics is often revered as a nebulous
subject best left to "experts" or simplified to populist pseudo-science.
This happens especially when economics is used without consideration of
the moral philosophy and socio-political context on which they are
founded.*

***Epic Economics** is a theatrical performance based on passages taken
from publications of distinguished economists from the 18th century to
today, highlighting their contributions and contradictions. Dimis
Michaelides weaves the words of the grand theorists with highlights and
stories from his own personal journey through economics and life. The
performance is supported by an original soundscape, humor, a few songs
and gentle interaction with spectators.*
